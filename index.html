NUST NET — Candidate Instructions
Computer Based Entry Test (CBNET) — Official Guidelines
📋 General Information
  • This is a Computer Based Entry Test (CBNET / NET) conducted by the National University of Sciences & Technology (NUST), Islamabad.
  • The test consists of 200 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) with a total duration of 3 hours (180 minutes).
  • Each correct answer carries 1 mark. There is NO negative marking for wrong answers.
  • The test is divided into sections based on your selected field / stream.
  • Candidates must attempt the test individually. Any form of assistance or collaboration is strictly prohibited.
📐 Test Structure by Field
  • NET-Engineering: Mathematics (100 MCQs) · Physics (60 MCQs) · English (40 MCQs)
  • NET-Applied Sciences: Biology (100 MCQs) · Chemistry (60 MCQs) · English (40 MCQs)
  • NET-Business & Social Sciences: Quantitative Mathematics (100 MCQs) · English (100 MCQs)
  • NET-Architecture / Industrial Design: Design Aptitude (100 MCQs) · Mathematics (60 MCQs) · English (40 MCQs)
  • NET-Natural Sciences: Mathematics (100 MCQs) · English (100 MCQs)
🖥️ How to Navigate the Test
  • Click on any option to select your answer. Your selection will be highlighted immediately.
  • Press Save to record your answer permanently and advance to the next question.
  • Use the Next (>) and Prev (<) buttons to move between questions freely.
  • Click on any number in the Navigation Grid to jump directly to that question.
  • Use the subject buttons (e.g., MATH, PHYS) at the bottom right to jump to the beginning of a section.
  • You may revisit and change any answer at any time before submitting.
🎨 Navigation Button Colour Legend
  • Grey — Question not yet visited or answered.
  • Green — Answer saved successfully.
  • Yellow — Marked for review (flagged).
  • Red Border — Currently active / viewed question.
🚩 Review & Flagging
  • Click the Review button to flag a question for later review. Flagged questions appear in yellow.
  • Flagged questions are not automatically submitted — you must save your chosen answer separately.
  • You are encouraged to answer all questions, even if you are unsure, as there is no penalty for wrong answers.
⏱️ Timer & Submission
  • The countdown timer is displayed at the bottom left of the screen throughout the test.
  • When time reaches 00:00:00, the test will be automatically submitted.
  • You may click "Click here to FINISH Test" at any time to submit early after confirmation.
  • Once submitted, you cannot return to the test. All answers are final.
⚠️ Aggregate Formula Reminder: Your final NUST merit is calculated as: 75% NET Score + 15% FSc/A-Level Marks + 10% Matric/O-Level Marks. Use the Aggregate Calculator on the next screen to estimate your merit.
🚫 Important Rules: Do not refresh or close the browser during the test. Do not use any unfair means. NUST reserves the right to cancel the candidature of any candidate found violating examination rules. Results shown at the end are for practice purposes only.

Please read all instructions carefully before proceeding.

NUST Official Formula (A-Level / O-Level):

NUST converts A-Level and O-Level grades to equivalent marks using the IBCC Equivalence system:

A-Level Grade → IBCC Marks (per subject):
A* = 90  |  A = 85  |  B = 75  |  C = 65  |  D = 55  |  E = 45

O-Level Grade → IBCC Marks (per subject):
A* = 90  |  A = 85  |  B = 75  |  C = 65  |  D = 55  |  E = 45
